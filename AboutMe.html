My Background

Hello! I am a Data Science professional and researcher with a foundational background in accounting and finance. I recently graduated Magna Cum Laude from Arizona State University with a Bachelor of Science in Data Science (Social Sciences track) and a minor in Business. My professional journey is driven by a distinct philosophy: true excellence in data science does not stem from programming syntax alone, but from the ability to navigate the final miles of the analytical lifecycle—interpreting what the figures actually mean and articulating those narratives to stakeholders with absolute clarity.

My analytical mindset was built at the intersection of industry experience and competitive academic research. I serve as a Staff Accountant at Personal Mark Accounting and Consulting, where I manage corporate financial records and build operational efficiencies. In tandem, I have pushed the boundaries of complex data pipeline engineering—ranging from co-authoring a peer-reviewed publication on Graph Neural Networks during a highly competitive National Science Foundation (NSF REU) research fellowship, to deploying machine learning pipelines for multi-variable biological risk modeling.

I view data science as a collaborative tool for strategic decision-making. Whether I am building predictive models, optimizing spatial routing algorithms, or leading financial budgeting operations as an executive officer, my core focus remains centered on data storytelling, rigorous research methods, and the ethical, transparent application of AI frameworks to ensure trusted, high-impact outcomes.
